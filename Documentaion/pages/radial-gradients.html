#Introduction

This file colorize-basics-radial allows you to create radial backgrounds it's super easy lets see how

First lets take a look at the color names available in this file because it's important for us to create the gradients, we have :

1- black 2- white 3- red
4- green 5- blue 6- yellow
7- orange 8- purple 9- pink
10- brown 11- gray 12- gold
13- maroon 14- aqua 15- turquoise
16- lightblue 17- lightgreen 18- lightgray

We have 18 different colors. To create a radial gradient we can mix any two colors together to generate the gradient

Notice : You can't mix more than two colors together also you can't mix the color with itself

So in total we can generate 153 different radial gradient if we take the inverse of them in count the total will be 306 different gradient.

#How it works ?

To create a radial gradient you should use a class that follows this convention grr-color1-color2

grr : stands for gradient radial
color1 : here you specify the first color in your mix
color2 : here you specify the second color in your mix

lets say you want to mix black and white your class should be grr-black-white for simplicity you don't have to memorize the order of the colors in your class so

grr-black-white = grr-white-black

They both will give you the exact same result take a look :

grr-white-black
grr-black-white

but what if you want to reverse the order of the mix you want white in the center and black on the outer side simple use the inverse flag

So you could use either one of these :

grr-black-white-inverse
grr-white-black-inverse Here is a sample :

grr-white-black-inverse
grr-black-white-inverse

#Tips :

  1. We Suggest that you use these type of gradients for buttons and small elements
